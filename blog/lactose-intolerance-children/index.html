Is your child lactose intolerant? Signs to watch for

Lactose intolerance happens when the body doesn't produce enough lactase — the enzyme that breaks down the natural sugar in milk. It's far more common than many parents realise, especially in Asian populations.

Common symptoms after dairy

If your child consistently shows any of these within 30 minutes to 2 hours of having milk or dairy, lactose may be the cause:

  • Bloating or a visibly swollen tummy
  • Tummy aches or cramping
  • Diarrhea or loose stools
  • Excess gas, burping or flatulence
  • Unusual fussiness or irritability after meals
  • Nausea or, occasionally, vomiting

Lactose intolerance vs. milk allergy

These two are often confused, but they're different. Lactose intolerance is a digestive issue. A milk allergy is an immune reaction and can cause hives, swelling, wheezing or — in severe cases — anaphylaxis. If you ever suspect an allergy, see a doctor right away.

What to do next

  1. Track symptoms. Keep a short food diary for a week, noting what your child eats and when symptoms appear.
  2. Try a short trial. Remove dairy for two weeks and see whether symptoms improve.
  3. Talk to your paediatrician before making long-term changes — kids still need calcium and protein from somewhere.

Lactose-free alternatives

From plant milks to fortified juices, there are now many ways to get the same nutrients without the discomfort. NOMI NOMI is naturally lactose-free, making it a friendly daily option for kids who get tummy troubles from milk-based drinks.
